Iterative Design Process

Identify the Problem

Seek to understand a given problem that can be solved through technological developments

Research

Approach the topic from all angles, conduct initial user interviews, and define a target audience. Develop user personas based on interviews.

Generate Ideas

List out all potential ideas. Begin sketching out site frameworks and content, then iterate over with increasingly detailed wireframes.

Test Sketches/Wireframes

Find ideal user group and conduct user interviews with wireframes. Give structured tasks to complete and observe responses, facial expressions, and feedback

Design Based on User Tests

Create a higher-fidelity prototype or web framework based on the user interviews

Test Designs

Conduct Nielson heuristic evaluations on another set of user interviews with the new models

Design Based on Heuristics

Re-design based on heuristic evaluations and additional user interviews

Repeat!

Design is a continuous cycle

Projects

Les Femmes de Substance

This is a website remodel I did as part of a team for Les Femmes de Substance, a pre-professional organization for women of color that encourages others to pursue leadership roles, develop their professional skills, and foster a sense of community. The website should be informative for the potential members and communicate any future events to the public. Some of their key goals are promoting leadership and unity, uplift other women, and create unity to combat the issues facing women of color.

Problem

Conducted an initial interview of the organization's e-baord to determine the current problems that they are facing. Client feels that student body is not aware of organization as a whole, their mission, and events they hold throughout the year. Client also wants to attract prospective members and increase event involvement. Client would like to make the website more interactive.

Research

Based on the information collected from initial interviews, we identified the client's target audience as: other professional organizations, external companies looking to partner up with Les Femmes, as well as other women seeking to get involved in the professional world, and two personas based on these observations. Our personas were students currently at Cornell

Ideas

With the persona in mind, we compiled a list of the client's wants and needs, then developed potential design solutions we could use to meet them. Some ideas to meet client needs include strong club branding through colors and images of club events to illustrate the idea that they are a professional organization yet personable to anyone who wants to learn more about the Les Femmes and include a President's page and information so that club appears more accessible and friendly We first tested these ideas before implementing them with persona walkthroughs

All About Orchids

Problem

This is a simple website I programmed as part of my intermediate design and programming class (INFO 2300). We were instructed to design a website about a personal interest that catered to a specific target persona. For this site I used HTML, CSS, and PHP

Research

For this project, the professor assigned us personas. My persona, Abby, was a middle-aged woman who lived as an accountant in New Jersey. She was reluctant to learn new technology, but is entirely capable of doing so. Abby likes to follow directions and is hestiant to step out of her comfort zone.

Ideas

The target audience for the site includes those like Abby who are keen on directions and have interest in lowkey hobbies such as gardening. The site is formatted so that information is easily accessed. Pages are organized on a navigation bar at the top of the webpage and split into "Home", "Care", "Types", and "Breeding." Because Abby does not like tinkering with new software and is more comfortable with the skills she currently has, the website is very straightforward that does not require a lot of experimenting.

FashionForward

Problem

My website is a fashion site where users can upload images of trends or outfits that they find interesting. This is a project I did for INFO 2300 (Intermediate Design and Programming for the Web). For this project, I used server-side and client-side programming to instate log-in functionality and image databases. Languages used include: HTML, CSS, PHP, and SQL

Research

My persona was a middle-aged woman named Abby, who is reluctant to step out of her comfort zone technology-wise. Because I was not very familiar with databases, I turned to online resources to brush up on SQL and database concepts. I drew inspiration for social media sites such as Instagram with the social network functionality.

Ideas

As Abbie is someone who is unlikely to step out of her comfort zone, I wanted to design the site to be as intuitive as possible. I used hover elements to indicate which links are clickable, and easy to read button for the forms so that Abbie can understand what fields to fill out and what they would do. The navigation bar changes color based on which page the user is on, and all links change colors so that the user can understand which ones are clickable. There are clear page titles so that Abbie can see which page she is on as well. Overall, the site is designed for ease of use so that users like Abbie can easily navigate the pages.
